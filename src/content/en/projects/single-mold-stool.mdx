The Single-Mold Stool asks a simple question: how much can you take away from a
stool before it stops being one? Here the answer is *almost everything* — every
part, fastener, and assembly step collapses into a single molded form.

![Single-mold stool, three-quarter view](https://picsum.photos/seed/stool-hero/1600/1000)

## Context

Most stools are an exercise in joinery — a seat, four legs, a few stretchers,
and the fasteners that hold them together. Every part adds tooling, inventory,
and one more chance for the object to loosen, wobble, or fail. The brief was to
remove that fragility at its source.

## Process

Starting from a single draft angle, the geometry was tuned so the part releases
cleanly from one mold while staying rigid underfoot. The leg taper is structural,
not decorative.

### Stacking

That same taper does double duty: it stiffens the legs and lets finished stools
nest into one another, so a dozen ship and store in the footprint of a few.

## Outcome

One mold, one material, one part. Nothing to tighten, nothing to lose — the
manufacturing logic and the object are the same decision.
